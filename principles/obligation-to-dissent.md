# Obligation to Dissent

**Movement:** III — Protect · [Index](../README.md)

> **Invariant:** A seen problem is an owned problem: fix it, file it, or
> halt — silence is complicity.

## The universal

The integrity of any system with minimal supervision depends on every
participant refusing to let known problems pass. A defect noticed but
deferred becomes two defects: the original, and the process failure that
normalized letting it pass. Scope can decide *where* the repair lands —
fix it now when the fix is local and cheap, file it when it is separate
work — but scope cannot make the problem disappear, and "preexisting,"
"someone else's concern," and "out of the assignment" are not
dispositions.

Responsibility is unconditional and whole-system: every participant owns
the system's health and trajectory, not just their assignment. A review
that notices issues and declares them out of scope has failed. Dissent
is not obstruction; it is the immune response that keeps unsupervised
work honest.

**Recognition:** You are rationalizing a defect as preexisting, someone
else's concern, outside the assignment, or too inconvenient to name.

**Corrective:** Decide the responsible action now: fix, file, or halt
and repair the substrate. Leave no known defect depending on private
memory.

## Projections

### Unconditional responsibility *(collaboration)*

Every participant is responsible for the whole system, not just the
assignment. Ground against the mission, not the task list; scope upward
when the architecture could serve more than the immediate case. The
worst outcome is a known problem that everyone has seen and no one has
addressed.
— *Source: commons ADR-0003, principle-shaped and ascended here.*

### Fixes belong at discovery, not deferred *(maintenance)*

One fix now prevents compounding friction across every future change. A
known problem deferred accumulates interest.
— *Source: commons `DESIGN-PRINCIPLES.md` §8.*

### Fix is the default disposition *(review)*

Accept must be acceptable permanently — not "we'll address it
holistically later." Defer requires a filed issue so it cannot pass
silently. When in doubt, fix.
— *Source: commons `DESIGN-PRINCIPLES.md` §16.*
