# Dosed Compliance

**Named composition** · [Index](../README.md)

> **Invariant:** The goal is compliance, not the gate. Pursue it from a
> menu of qualitatively different levers — structural enforcement is
> one — dosed by rule of thumb: the easy and elegant freely; the
> extreme and costly only for a compelling reason.

Long form: *a desired property is produced from a menu, not maximized
through a gate.* A calibration discipline composed of three existing
universals. Each constituent's content lives in its own document —
consult them by link; this document owns only what no constituent
holds: the menu, and the rule of thumb as the only available economics.

## The composition

- **The goal is the property, not the mechanism** —
  [Grounding](../principles/grounding.md). A gate is an instrument; the
  need is that the property holds. Targeting maximal enforcement is the
  proxy failure Grounding names: satisfying the instrument instead of
  the thing it measures.
- **The dose** — [Parsimony](../principles/parsimony.md). An extreme or
  costly lever is an element like any other: it earns its place by a
  verified constraint or it is not built. Cost is part of the normal
  calibration, not a binary escape hatch bolted on at the end.
- **The floor** — [Evolvability](../principles/evolvability.md). Dosing
  is not license to under-build: when the scale ahead will exceed what
  can be pictured, a hand-policed convention is a debt instrument, and
  debt does not flow forward. The dose rises to the worthwhile
  frontier — the rung where the next increment of enforcement costs
  more than it returns — and no further.

## What the composition itself owns

**The menu.** The inherited error is treating enforcement as a single
dial from hand-policed to structurally-enforced. Compliance is pursued
from a menu of qualitatively different levers, most of which are not
enforcement at all. Naming the menu is what makes the discipline
actionable: it says where to *look* before reaching for the costly
gate. The live working set — non-exhaustive, extensible by use:

- **Inherit an existing solution.** A compliance problem is usually a
  known member of a general family with mature solutions — ecosystem
  tools, lints, established patterns. Research first; the
  highest-value first move.
- **Documentation.** Often the cheapest real compliance gain.
- **Context and disposition.** Improve what the actor *sees* — context
  management, principle injection — so compliance arises from
  disposition, upstream of any gate.
- **Structural enforcement.** A machine-checkable guard — one lever
  among many, reached for as far as it pays, not reflexively to the
  maximum.
- **Gamification.** State the compliance property as a win condition,
  so an actor optimizes *toward* it rather than being blocked *by* it.

**The rule of thumb as the only available economics.** The marginal
cost-vs-benefit computation cannot actually be performed — the inputs
are not measurable. The economics cashes out only as the blunt, usable
heuristic in the invariant: easy and elegant, do it; extreme and
costly, only with a compelling reason. Stating it more precisely than
that would claim a calculation no one can run.

**Recognition:** A desired property's pursuit is written as "enforce
structurally unless something forbids it" — full enforcement as the
target, cost as a binary exception. Or you are reaching for a guard,
hook, or gate before surveying the cheaper levers.

**Corrective:** Name the property. Survey the menu, cheapest lever
first. Climb the cost scale only while the next rung visibly pays, and
stop at the worthwhile frontier.

## Failure diagnostic

Each missing constituent corrupts the discipline in a named way:

- *Without Grounding:* the gate becomes the goal — enforcement built
  for its own sake, theater that satisfies the instrument while the
  property drifts.
- *Without Parsimony:* over-dose — every convention gets a guard, and
  the system calcifies under its own police.
- *Without Evolvability:* under-dose — hand-policed conventions erode
  as the surface multiplies, and the debt lands on more future hands.

## Relations

- **composed-of** — [Grounding](../principles/grounding.md) ·
  [Parsimony](../principles/parsimony.md) ·
  [Evolvability](../principles/evolvability.md). Each constituent
  carries the reciprocal **composition** edge. Evolvability's *scale
  honesty* corollary was the corpus's first instance of the
  maximized-gate shape this composition corrects; it now states the
  dosed frontier
  ([#7](https://github.com/pentaxis93/principles/issues/7)).
- **kin (boundary)** —
  [Verifiable Completion](../principles/verifiable-completion.md): the
  dose decides whether a gate is built; Verifiable Completion governs
  what any claim of completion must do regardless. Its evidence demand
  takes no dosed qualifier, because evidence is constitutive of a
  completion claim — not a lever toward a property — and the principle
  already admits the cheapest sufficient evidence form (inspection,
  command, artifact shape): the menu inside the evidence demand.
